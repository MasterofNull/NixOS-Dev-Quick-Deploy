## Review receipt contract

`aq.review-round-receipt.v1` is closed and bounded. It binds:

- round/pass ID, pass index, same-baseline ID/hash, expert-role set, criteria hash, and subject hash;
- immutable sorted-path-plus-raw-SHA subject package hash, roster snapshot hash, review-policy hash and
  version, required/optional membership, binding count, minimum independent principals/families, and
  eligibility source;
- lane identity, model artifact/profile/family, execution principal, assigned role, lineage, and
  review eligibility (`advisory`, `binding_flagship`, `recused`);
- lane state (`submitted`, `failed`, `timed_out`, `parked`, `unavailable`) distinct from an explicit
  `abstain` verdict;
- per-criterion result, privacy-safe evidence references, reproduced checks, residual risk, and
  exact typed terminal verdict;
- implementer/material-rewriter lineage, independence result, revision/supersession hashes, expiry,
  and invalidation on subject/criteria/baseline drift.

Receipt terminal decisions are `accepted`, `revision_required`, `rejected`, or `incomplete`. The total
mapping from existing contribution/reviewer surfaces is:

```text
APPROVE | PASS -> pass
APPROVE_WITH_CHANGES | REQUEST_REVISION -> revision_required
REJECT | FAIL -> fail
ABSTAIN -> abstain (submitted verdict; never unavailable and never quorum)
```

Only structured typed input maps. Prose/fallback extraction cannot count toward binding acceptance.
Schemas are generated from the Pydantic SSOT and checked for deterministic byte equality.

Existing lane-state mapping is total:

```text
pending | dispatching | running -> nonterminal; required lane makes receipt incomplete
submitted -> terminal accounting; verdict required
failed | timed_out -> terminal accounting; no quorum
amended -> terminal accounting only when its exact amended subject hash is present
parked | unavailable -> C0.5 receipt extension; terminal accounting; no quorum
```

Terminal-decision precedence is policy-frozen: any undisposed critical dissent or nonterminal required
lane yields `incomplete`; any eligible fail yields `rejected`; any eligible revision verdict or
disposed critical dissent requiring change yields `revision_required`; only complete roster accounting,
binding quorum, no blocking finding, and all binding passes yields `accepted`; otherwise `incomplete`.

Pure adjudication is policy-driven and fails closed when a required lane has no typed terminal reason,
baselines, roster/policy/criteria, or subject hashes differ, or the configured binding quorum is not
met. `failed`, `timed_out`, `parked`, and `unavailable` satisfy terminal accounting but never quorum;
`abstain` is a submitted verdict. Independence is computed from trusted dispatch/assignment lineage,
never reviewer assertion: dispatch request/task, execution principal, model artifact/family/profile,
implementer, and every material rewriter. A material edit changes the subject hash, recuses the editor,
invalidates old receipts, binds predecessor/supersedes, and rejects cycles or hash reuse.

Advisory local/economical evidence remains non-binding. However, any fresh attributable critical
advisory dissent forces `incomplete` before a binding reviewer records a typed disposition bound to
the finding hash. After disposition, the policy-derived decision is `revision_required`, `rejected`,
or remains `incomplete`; it cannot become `accepted` without resolving the finding. Substantive
rejects cannot be silently overridden.

## Learning candidate contract

`aq.learning-candidate.v1` is the single taxonomy SSOT. It covers findings from runtime, validation,
review, security, monitoring, routing, capacity, tool use, local hardware/model behavior, and recovery.
It binds original failure and successful retry separately, reproduction/evidence, severity,
deduplication identity, affected consumers, proposed candidates, evaluation/canary/rollback evidence,
and disposition.

Evidence includes immutable artifact/source-event hashes, provenance/trust class, independent verifier
identity, sanitizer/redaction state, and deterministic dedup derivation. Mutable paths, raw prose,
unverified references, self-authored eval/canary evidence, and evaluator/candidate co-authorship without
independent verification fail closed.

Closed affected-consumer taxonomy:

```text
shared_contract · flagship_prompt · implementer_prompt · local_agent_profile
local_logic_template · embedded_corpus · skill_or_instruction · tool_contract
routing_policy · evaluator · regression_fixture · monitoring_rule
```

Lifecycle:

```text
captured -> triaged -> fixture_bound -> candidate_prepared -> shadow_validated
-> flagship_accepted -> canary -> promoted | rolled_back
captured|triaged|fixture_bound -> non_propagated (typed rationale required)
```

No transition mutates a consumer. The module returns promotion intent/evidence only. Promotion requires
the exact candidate, fixture/evaluator, accepted review receipt, canary/soak, rollback-target, and
source-event hashes; independent flagship acceptance; consumer-projection freshness plan; and CAS
expected revision. The caller injects `evaluated_at`; the module has no clock. Raw findings, one-off
successes, or model prose cannot modify prompts, skills, tools, routing, corpora, memory, or weights.

Local modalities are closed as `agentic_coding`, `bounded_logic`, and `embedded_retrieval`. Hardware/
model profile, quantization, context/budget, phase timeouts, and tools are capability provenance only.
Embedded retrieval may supply evidence references but can never be a lane, role, vote, verdict,
quorum member, or lineage identity. Local capacity failures are typed separately from abstention.

## Golden vectors and acceptance

Schemas bound all IDs, SHA-256 values, RFC3339 timestamps, reasons, evidence refs, strings, arrays,
integers, and maps; nested objects are closed, arrays are unique/bounded, and semantic validators
enforce cross-field/independence rules not expressible in JSON Schema.

Fixtures cover: same-baseline full roster; mismatched baseline; subject/criteria/policy/roster drift;
permutation invariance and Unicode normalization; exact boundary sizes and max-plus-one; malformed
hash/timestamp/nested unknowns; advisory local
dissent; explicit abstention; unavailable versus abstaining; failed, timed-out, and parked flagship;
self-review and forged `self_review=false`; material rewriter recusal; insufficient binding quorum;
duplicate lane/principal/family under aliases; superseded hash; revision cycle/hash reuse;
typed revision; exact terminal verdict; finding deduplication; successful retry preserving failure;
critical advisory disposition; each affected consumer; missing fixture; direct-mutation attempt;
poisoned/mutable evidence; candidate/evaluator co-authorship; embedded vote rejection; local modality/
capacity cases; shadow failure; flagship self-acceptance; missing canary/rollback; CAS replay; valid
promotion; rollback; typed non-propagation; and stable canonical output/hash.

Acceptance requires:

1. Draft 2020-12 closed schemas with bounded strings, arrays, integers, and nested objects.
2. Pydantic/schema projections agree and unknown fields fail closed.
3. Existing round golden/aggregate/decision suites remain green.
4. Exact deterministic outcomes for every golden vector.
5. No filesystem mutation, subprocess, provider, network, clock, randomness, or live authority import
   in the pure module.
6. C0 lane/model-tier/privacy taxonomy is reused or explicitly versioned; review lanes bind dispatch
   task/idempotency/request evidence, with legacy/manual provenance explicitly low-trust.
7. Python/JSON validation, doc links, focused suites, and exact-staged Tier0 pass.
8. Same-baseline flat review contributions are recorded; independent binding flagship acceptance of
   the exact candidate is required before commit.
