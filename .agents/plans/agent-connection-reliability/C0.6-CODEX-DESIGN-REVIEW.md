## Blocking revisions

### 1. Reconcile the 180-second vector with the review-policy cap

Section 7 says `--timeout 180` remains 180 seconds, while section 4.3 correctly caps the current
research/review mapping at 60 seconds and rejects a larger explicit request as `admission_denied` with
`requested_budget_above_policy`. Split this into two deterministic vectors: the incident-shaped
research/review request must reject before queue/provider work, while a separately named eligible task
class, caller tier, and profile must prove that an accepted explicit deadline is immutable and is not
expanded by a 4,096-token estimate or legacy environment value.

### 2. Bind writer-lease sequencing to R0.1

Track A and the active R0.1 seven-file lease both include
`scripts/testing/harness_qa/phases/phase0.py`. There is no current collision because C0.6 is design-only
and Track A is dormant. Add an explicit gate that Track A authorization/implementation cannot begin
until R0.1 is terminally closed and fresh predecessor hashes are frozen. C0.6-T must also compare its
exact inventory with the R0.1 foundation and later web-visibility leases before implementation.

### 3. Freeze quantitative activation and rollback thresholds

The packet requires a canary/soak and refers to an “accepted threshold” without requiring an artifact
to bind it. Require C0.6-T or the later activation packet to freeze minimum sample count and soak
duration, zero deadline overruns, zero stale owners/audit gaps/unexplained running rows, maximum
dashboard visibility delay, expected classifications for injected timeouts, and the maximum unplanned
timeout/incomplete-output rate that triggers rollback. Missing or failed measurements must block live
activation.

## Parallel-work boundary

Safe in parallel:

- R0.1 completion and independent acceptance;
