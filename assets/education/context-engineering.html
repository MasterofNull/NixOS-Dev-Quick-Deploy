Token Budgets & Prompt Planning

Large Language Models are limited by their attention mechanisms. Simply reading entire folders wastes tokens and increases input latency. Furthermore, research proves that LLMs degrade in accuracy when critical facts are buried in the middle of long prompts.

Our AI operating system utilizes a **"Lazy Context Injection"** strategy: loading only the indices and signatures of skills initially, mapping search keywords, and reading file bodies solely during the execution phase.

Attentional Performance and RAG Design

The Lost in the Middle Phenomenon: LLMs retrieve information at the beginning or end of input sequences with highest accuracy. We structure prompt templates to place the critical system constraints (user rules, safety keys, API options) at the absolute bottom or top of the payload.

Compression: Using techniques like token summary caching allows us to preserve working memory across agent tasks while avoiding redundancy.
